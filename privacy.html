Privacy

Your thoughts stay
on your phone.

This is not merely a policy position. It is how the product is built. There is no server receiving your captures, no cloud storing your voice memos, and no account to sign into. We could not read your reminders if we wanted to.

The one exception is the CONNECTED plan. If you choose to share something with a specific other Captrieve person, that share travels through our servers encrypted with a key held only on your device and theirs, so that even we cannot read it. That is the whole story, in a sentence. The formal policy below says the same thing in more detail, and in the same plain language.

The formal policy

Effective [EFFECTIVE DATE]. Captrieve is operated by [LEGAL ENTITY] ("we", "us"). You can reach us at support@captrieve.com. Where this page and any shorter summary ever seem to disagree, the practices described here govern.

What stays on your device

On your phone,
and only your phone.

The following are created and stored locally on your device. We never receive them, and they are not backed up to any server we control.

Your captures

Text notes, photos, and voice recordings. Voice is transcribed on-device, without engaging any backend service.

Your places

When you designate a place such as home or a pharmacy, the app stores what it needs to recognize it again. That can include a map location, the name of a Wi-Fi network you are connected to, a Bluetooth device name, or an NFC tag identifier. You can see exactly what is stored for each place, and remove any of it, in the app. We never look a place up in any outside database, and we never guess your places from your movements. You tell us, or we do not know.

Your cues and their history

The reminders you set, the conditions that fire them, and the on-device log of what fired and when.

Diagnostic logs

The app keeps a log on your device to help fix problems. It records only the shape of what happened, such as which kind of cue fired, never your locations or the content of your reminders. Separately, a detailed log that can include location is turned off by default. If you turn it on to diagnose a problem, it stays on your device, turns itself off again after a day, and is sent to us only if you deliberately submit a problem report. The information we do collect to improve the app is described in the next section.

What we collect to improve the app

No email, no name,
no Apple or Google login.

Captrieve is shaped entirely by you, and all of that lives on your phone. We are the ones who never learn who you are. There is no account to create, so we never ask for your email, and we never receive your Apple or Google sign-in. To improve the app we collect a small amount of content-free information about how it is working, none of it tied to your identity. It is on by default, and you can turn it off at any time.

What we collect

Which kinds of cues fire and how often, which features get used, and crash and error reports. In other words, the shape of how the app performs, so we can see what works and fix what breaks.

When something goes wrong, we also include the recent sequence of steps that led up to it, because some bugs only appear after a particular order of events. Those steps are the same content-free kind: which screen, which action, which kind of cue, in what order, never what your reminder said or where you were.

What we never collect

We never ask for or receive your email address, your name, your phone number, or your Apple or Google sign-in. There is no account to create and no profile to build. We also never collect the content of your captures and reminders, your photos or voice, or your locations. We do not track you across other apps, and we share nothing with advertisers or data brokers.

A random ID, not your identity

To tell reports apart, the information is grouped by a random ID your phone creates the first time you run the app. It is not your name, not an account (there is none), and not any identifier built into your device. We cannot turn it back into you. It only lets us see that the same install hit a problem several times, or that two errors tend to happen together, which is what makes the reports useful enough to act on. It resets if you reinstall the app, and turning diagnostics off stops sending it.

Why we collect it

To see which features people actually use, to find the cues that misbehave, and to learn about crashes we would otherwise never hear about. It is the difference between guessing and knowing what to improve for you.

Where to turn it off

In the app, under Settings, then Privacy, then Diagnostics. One switch turns off all of it immediately, and the app works exactly the same.

What we cannot see

Not "we promise not to."
"We cannot."

A promise asks you to trust us. Some of these are stronger than a promise, so we label which is which. Some things are physically impossible for any app. Some are prevented by Apple and Google. And some are our commitment, kept by how the product is built.

Your internet activity (physically impossible)

Captrieve is not a VPN and requests no network-monitoring permission. The phone's design does not let an ordinary app see what other apps send or what you browse.

Your Wi-Fi passwords (prevented by the platform)

iOS and Android never hand a saved network password to an app. There is nothing for us to store, and no way for us to read it. When the app uses Wi-Fi to recognize a place, it uses only the network's name.

The networks around you (prevented by the platform)

On iPhone, an app cannot get a list of nearby Wi-Fi networks at all. The app only ever knows the single network you are actively connected to, and only after you grant location permission, which the operating system requires before revealing even a network name.

Anything off your phone, by default (our commitment)

Because the app does have an internet connection for the CONNECTED plan, "nothing leaves your phone" is a commitment we keep by design, not a physical impossibility. Your captures, places, and cues are stored only on your device, and the only thing we transmit is a CONNECTED share you explicitly create, encrypted as described below.

Advertising and tracking (our commitment)

We include no advertising and no third-party analytics or tracking software. We do not build a profile of you, and we have no one to sell it to.

What moves, and only when you choose it

The CONNECTED plan,
and nothing else.

The FREE and SOLO tiers transmit nothing. The CONNECTED plan exists for one purpose, to route information between two people who have each chosen to be connected.

–

End-to-end encrypted What you share is encrypted on your device with a key held only on your device and your recipient's. It passes through our servers as ciphertext we cannot read, and neither we nor an attacker who breached our servers could decrypt it.

–

A conduit, not a brain Our server holds a share only long enough to deliver it to the device you sent it to, then it is gone. We do not analyze it, and we cannot, because it is encrypted.

–

Push to wake, with no content To deliver promptly, we use Apple Push Notification service and Firebase Cloud Messaging to wake the receiving app so it can fetch the share. The wake-up itself carries no readable content.

Apple and Google operate these push services, and your device's push token passes through them. They do not receive your captures or the content of your shares.

–

Disconnect stops it immediately Disconnecting from a person is a single action in the app and takes effect at once. Presence events stop, their access is revoked, and the data you shared is purged from our server. This is separate from billing. Canceling your subscription in the App Store or Google Play stops the renewal but does not end a connection, so use Disconnect when you need sharing to stop now.

Permissions, payments, and keeping or deleting your data

Permissions

The app asks only for permissions it needs to do what you ask of it. Location fires geofence cues and is used only for that. Microphone records voice captures, transcribed on-device. Camera and NFC capture photos and tag identifiers, stored locally. Bluetooth and Wi-Fi information let cues recognize a device or a network, by name only, stored locally. Notifications deliver the reminders you set.

Payments

Purchases are handled by the Apple App Store and Google Play under their own terms and privacy policies. We do not receive or store your payment card details.

Keeping and deleting your data

Because your data lives on your device, you control it directly. Deleting a capture, a place, or the app itself removes that data from your phone. On the CONNECTED plan, using Disconnect purges shared data from our server. Since we hold no account for you, there is no profile on our side to delete.

Children's privacy

Captrieve is not directed to children under [CHILDREN AGE], and we do not knowingly collect information from them. Because we operate no account and collect no personal data on our servers, the app's local-only design applies equally to every user.

Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data, and to object to its processing. For Captrieve these are largely satisfied by design, since your data is on your device and under your control. For any request or question, contact us at support@captrieve.com.
