Capture · Retrieve · Remember

The thought you have now is the thought future you will need.

Captrieve solves a single problem: you have a thought you do not want to lose and cannot act on right now, and you want to encounter it later, at a moment when it will be actionable.

A captured thought does not go into your memory, which was never designed to hold a half-formed idea until the right context arrives. It doesn't go into a Google doc, a spreadsheet, a notes app, a list app, or any place else where it passively waits for you to remember that it's there and go looking for it, unindexed. It doesn't go into a reminder app that only knows to mention things at a pre-defined date and time, but not based on what you are doing or where you are.

Captrieve makes your ideas reappear when and where you tell them to.

How it works

Three steps.
That's the whole thing.

Captrieve is not a notes app. It is not a reminder app. It does not want your attention. It wants to disappear into your life and show up when something matters.

1

Capture it

Tap, speak, done — before the thought is gone. Voice is primary because thoughts move faster than typing. Photo and text work too, for when the moment calls for it.

2

Tell it when or where

Set a time, a place, a Wi-Fi network, or a physical tag. When you leave work. When you arrive at the clinic. When you tap the tag on your piano bench. Tomorrow morning.

3

It finds you

At the right moment, in the right place, it surfaces. Not in a pile of notes you have to dig through. Not because an algorithm decided you were ready. Because you told it to.

Who is Captrieve for?
Anyone whose thoughts arrive at the wrong moment.

Creative people

Ideas don't wait for a convenient moment. Captrieve captures them at the speed they arrive and delivers them back when you are positioned to do something with them — at the studio, at the desk, in the flow state that makes the work happen.

People with ADHD

A reminder that fires at an arbitrary time is easy to dismiss and forget. A reminder that fires when you walk through the door of the place where it matters is a different thing entirely. Context-triggered retrieval is not a convenience for people with ADHD — it is a meaningful functional accommodation.

People managing their health and memory

The question you thought of on Tuesday needs to be there on Thursday when the doctor asks if you have anything else. Captrieve is a reliable external memory that surfaces things in exactly the context where they are useful — and everything stays private on your phone, always.

Families and caregivers

Set up retrieves for someone you care for. Know they are moving through their day. Share a thought that finds them at exactly the right moment. Learn more →
