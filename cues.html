Cues

The right moment
is different every time.

A thought about the grocery store is useless at 10pm and essential when you walk through the door. A question for your doctor needs to be there on Thursday, not on Tuesday when you thought of it. A melody needs to find you at the piano, not in the shower where it arrived. Captrieve has a cue for each of these — and many more.

Location

The place is the cue.
Three ways to tell it where.

GPS geofencing, Wi-Fi network detection, and NFC tags each solve a different version of the location problem. Use whichever fits the place — or combine them for the same location.

Geofence

When I arrive — or leave — somewhere

Draw a circle on a map. When your phone enters or exits that circle, the retrieve fires. Works anywhere with GPS signal — outdoors, in a city, at an airport approach. Set the radius from a single building to an entire neighborhood.

Combine with a time condition — "when I leave the office, but only after 5pm" — or add a delay: "20 minutes after I leave work, when I'm close enough to the store to stop."

Wi-Fi network

When I connect to a specific network

Joining a Wi-Fi network is a precise, passive location signal that GPS cannot match indoors. Your home network means you're home. The office network means you're at your desk. The dentist's network means you're in the waiting room.

No hardware needed. No battery drain. Just the networks you already connect to, doing double duty as location signals.

Home network Office network Airport lounge Joe's house

NFC tag — tap to check in

When I tap a tag I've placed somewhere

Small, inexpensive stickers you place anywhere. Tap your phone against one and whatever you've set for that location surfaces instantly. The tap is deliberate — a physical micro-ritual that asks: I am here now, what did I need?

NFC tags solve the indoor precision problem GPS and Wi-Fi can't. Your desk, your piano, your medicine cabinet, your front door — each becomes a precise, reliable context.

NFC tags in depth

A sticker, a tap,
a physical memory.

NFC tags cost less than a dollar each. You can buy them in packs of 30 for under $15. Stick them anywhere. Name them in Captrieve. Then tap.

Where people put them

At home: front door, back door, bedside table, kitchen counter, refrigerator, medicine cabinet, bathroom mirror, home office desk, reading chair.

Creative and hobby spaces: piano, guitar case, recording desk, easel, sewing machine, workbench.

Work: desk, monitor, conference room tables, filing cabinet.

On the go: car dashboard, bicycle handlebar, gym bag.

Disposable: airplane seat armrest, hotel desk, temporary locker. Tags are inexpensive enough to leave behind.

The airplane seat case

Stick a tag on the armrest when you board. Set a retrieve for anything you need to remember when you leave the plane. Tap the tag when you gather your things to depart.

A GPS geofence on an airport fires when you're in the parking lot, on approach, or dropping someone off. An NFC tap on departure is unambiguous. You're leaving. Right now.

The tap is a deliberate micro-ritual. I am here now. What did I need?

A thought on habits

Captrieve is designed to
make itself unnecessary.

Use the nightstand tag every night to remember your bedtime routine. After a while you notice you don't tap it anymore. The routine runs on its own. The place became the cue — not the app.

That is not a failure. That is the product working at its best. You just spent a few dollars on some stickers and permanently improved your memory in a way that had eluded you for years.

Keep the tags where they are. Tap them anyway. Something else worth remembering will come along.

More cue types

Time, context,
and what you're connected to.

Date and time

When a specific moment arrives

Tomorrow morning. This Friday at noon. The night before your flight. Simple and reliable — Siri and your calendar can do this too, but Captrieve composes it with location and context in ways they don't.

Bluetooth device

When I connect to a device

Connecting to your car audio means you're in the car. Connecting to your home speaker means you're home. Connecting to your work headset means you're at your desk. Devices you already own, doing double duty as context signals.

App open

Next time I open Captrieve

Not every thought has a clean time or place. Sometimes you just know you'll want it soon. This cue fires the next time you open the app — until you ask, the thought waits.

Calendar (Connected tier)

Before a specific meeting or person

"Before my next appointment with Dr. Reyes." "Before any meeting with the keyword 'budget'." Calendar integration resolves your captures to real events and updates them if the schedule changes. Available on the Connected tier.

Conditions

Not just where.
Where, and when, and only if.

Any cue can carry conditions that must also be true for the retrieve to fire. Leave the office, but only if it's after 5pm. Arrive at the grocery store, but only on a weekday.

And any capture can have multiple cues. Arrive at the store or tap the fridge tag — whichever comes first.
